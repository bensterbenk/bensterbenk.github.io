Hi! I’m Ben Sterbenk, a 3rd year undergraduate student at Ithaca College pursuing a double BA in Legal Studies and Computer Science.

I’m fascinated by the ways we can use the power of technology to advance legal, humanitarian, and creative development. In my opinion, the application of tech and computer science to the humanities and arts is of vital importance in our rapidly evolving world.

During my time at Ithaca College:

I’ve created:

  • A variety of coding projects in and out of class, in the process gaining experience of full-stack web development, Python, C++, JavaScript, and Java. To see these projects, you can click on the github icon on the left.

  • Essays, research papers and written arguments which expanded my abilities as a writer and thinker in my humanities coursework and in various other contexts. To read some of my work, you can click the book on the right.

Worked as:

  • a Legislative Intern for NYS Assemblymember Anna Kelles.

  • a Tech Lead at ICode Corning, where I had the pleasure of teaching computer science concepts, game development, and creative technology to elementary and middle school students.

And had other amazing experiences like:

  • Studying computer science at Universitat Pompeu Fabra in Barcelona, where I had the opportunity to learn about the intersection between computer science and audiovisual perception, as well as to advance my knowledge on Machine Learning and C++ development.

  • Representing the Ithaca College Model United Nations team at conferences in Harvard University and the University of Chicago, where I explored solutions to things like labor policy in an AI-dominated future and European Union economic issues with students from all over the world.

  • Exploring the amazing world of electronic music through the diverse artistic community of Ithaca by producing and engineering songs for talented local student artists at Cornell University and Ithaca College and develop soundtracks for film students.

In the future, I’m excited to continue to explore new and important applications of computer science and tech to law, politics, and the arts, and to improve upon my experience as a developer, writer, student and human.
